Paneer Butter Masala

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 tablespoons butter (salted or unsalted, divided)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 whole black cardamom
  • ¼ cup chopped red onions
  • 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1 inch piece of fresh ginger (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit into half)
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powde
  • ¼ cup tomato puree (or 2 tbsp tomato paste)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 1 pound paneer (500 g, cut into cubes)
  • 2 teaspoons honey
  • 1 pound paneer (500 g, cut into cubes)

RECIPE:

Heat tablespoon butter and vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
Once the butter is hot, add cloves, black cardamom, and green cardamoms and fry for 3-4 seconds.

Add tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and green chilies and cook for 2-3 minutes until the tomatoes are mushy.
Keep stirring frequently. Now add cashews Transfer the cooled mixture to a blender along with ¼ cup water and blend to make a smooth paste.
Heat the remaining 3 tablespoon butter in the same skillet over medium heat.
Add the ground paste to the skillet. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, Kashmiri red chili powder, tomato puree, tomato ketchup, and salt and stir until combined.
Add paneer cubes to the skillet and stir to coat the cubes with the gravy. Cook for 3-4 minutes.
Add some water if the gravy is too thick. Add garam masala spice mix, heavy cream, honey, and Kasuri methi. Cook for another minute.
Check for salt and add more if required.
Transfer the curry to the serving bowl. Garnish with heavy cream swirls and serve hot.


Punjabi Dal Tadka

INGREDIENTS

  • ½ cup yellow pigeon peas (arhar dal, toor dal) (or pink masoor)
  • ¼ cup skinless petite yellow lentils (yellow moong dal)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • ½ cup chopped onions
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
  • 1 teaspoon chopped green chilies
  • ½ cup chopped tomatoes
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro (fresh coriander leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (replace with oil for vegan dal)
  • 2-3 whole dry red chilies

RECIPE:

Add ginger and garlic and fry for 2 minutes. Add green chilies and tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Stir frequently while cooking. Now add coriander powder, turmeric powder, and Kashmiri red chili powder, and cook for a minute.

Drain the water from the lentils and add them to the pot along with 3 cups of water, and salt, and stir well.
Press PRESSURE COOK and set the timer to 10 minutes on high pressure.
Add lime juice to the pot and mix the cooked dal well using a wire whisk or a madani until it is smooth and creamy.
Add some more water if the dal is thick for your liking and bring it to a boil by pressing the SAUTE button. Check for salt and add more if needed. Set the cooked dal aside.
Once the oil is hot and shimmery, add onions and fry until they turn light brown in color (8-10 minutes),
Add ginger and garlic and fry for 2 minutes. Add green chilies and tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Now add coriander powder, turmeric powder, and Kashmiri red chili powder, and cook for a minute. Add the dal to the pot along with 3 cups of water, and salt and stir well.
Remove the cooker from heat and let the pressure release naturally. Open the lid of the cooker.
Add lime juice to the pot and mix the cooked dal well using a wire whisk or a madani until it is smooth and creamy. Add some more water if the dal is thick for your liking and bring it to a boil.
Once the lentils are cooked, let’s give them a tempering.
Heat ghee in a small pan over medium-high heat. Once the ghee is hot, add cumin seeds and asafetida and let them crackle for 4-5 seconds.
Add dry red chilies and garlic and fry until it turns golden brown in color. Pour the tempering immediately over the dal and mix well. Garnish with cilantro and serve hot.


Punjabi Chole Masala

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup dried chole (white chickpeas) (7 oz, 200 g)
  • 2 teaspoons black tea leaves (chai patti) (or 2 tea bags)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2-3 cloves (laung)
  • 1 whole black cardamom (badi elaichi)
  • 5 tablespoons vegetable oil (or mustard oil, ghee)
  • 1 cup grated onions
  • 2 teaspoons ginger garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons chole masala powder
  • 1 cup grated fresh tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon anardana powder (pomegranate seed powder) (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon julienned ginger
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit into half)

RECIPE:

Wash chickpeas with water and soak them in 3-4 cups of water for 4-5 hours. Heat water in a pan over medium heat.

Lightly crush cloves, black cardamom, green cardamoms, and cinnamon in a mortar and pestle. Heat oil in a pressure cooker over medium-high heat.
Add onions and fry until they turn slightly brown in color, stirring frequently (8-10 minutes).
Add ginger garlic paste and fry for 2 minutes. Now add tomatoes and cook for another minute, stirring frequently.
Add chole masala powder, coriander powder, chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and anardana powder . Now add the soaked chole, tea water, baking soda, salt, and 1 cup of plain water to the cooker and stir gently.
Add Kauri methi and tamarind paste and mix well. Slightly mash the chole using the back of a ladle for a super creamy texture of the curry.
Press SAUTE mode and follow the step until closing the lid. Close the lid of the instant pot and set the valve to the sealing position. Now press PRESSURE COOK mode and set the timer to 20 minutes at high pressure.
Once the timer goes off, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes. Add Kauri methi and tamarind paste and mix well.
Slightly mash the chole using the back of a ladle for a super creamy texture for the curry. Check for salt and add more if needed.


Punjabi Missi Roti

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup Chickpea flour
  • ½ cup Whole wheat flour
  • ½ cup All purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • ¼ cup Corn Flour
  • 2 tablespoon Oil and salt
  • 2 teaspoon Kasuri methi
  • 1 teaspoon Green chilli (Finely chopped)
  • ½ inch Ginger (Grated)
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 2 tablespoon Onion (Finely Chopped)
  • Ghee for applying on top

RECIPE:

Add all the ingredients in a bowl and mix nicely with your finger tips. Add little water and knead to make a soft dough.
Cover the dough with a kitchen towel and keep aside for ½ an hour. Dry roast coriander seeds and cumin seeds until slightly browned. Crush them in a mortar and pestle to make coarse powder.
Mix this powder with onion and fresh coriander. Make a smooth ball from each part.
Press the dough ball into the topping mixture. Keep the topping side down and dust and roll the dough ball to a 4 inch disc.
Apply water on the top of the roti. Transfer the roti with the water side down on the griddle.
Turn the griddle upside down and coo the roti on direct flame until brown spots appear. Keep moving the roti to roast it from all over. Turn the griddle and cook the roti for another minute.
Apply ghee on top and remove it from the griddle. Serve hot with any curry or dal fry


Butter Chicken

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 pound boneless chicken breasts (500 g)
  • 2 tablespoons thick yogurt (or Greek yogurt)
  • ½ cup chopped onions
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2 teaspoons Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 3 tablespoons butter (salted or unsalted)
  • 2 ounce processed cheese (50 g, shredded)
  • 2 ounce khoya (50 g, crumbled)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons thick yogurt (or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • 3 tablespoons butter (salted or unsalted)
  • 2 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 inch piece of cinnamon stick

RECIPE:

Wash the chicken breasts and drain any extra water. Mix the chicken pieces with thick yogurt, coriander powder, lime juice, and salt.
The longer the chicken marinates, the softer and juicier it will turn out. Arrange the marinated chicken on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil. Roast for 25-30 minutes until the chicken is nicely browned and tender.
Note – You can broil the ready chicken for 5 minutes to get that charred color and flavor. Note – You can also grill the marinated chicken in a skillet over the stovetop. Make the Gravy
Heat 4 cups of water in a pan over medium-high heat. When it comes to a boil, add cashew nuts, tomatoes, onions, whole green cardamoms, whole black cardamoms, cinnamon stick, cloves, and whole black peppercorns.
Drain the water and add the boiled ingredients to a blender along with ½ cup of water. Blend to make a smooth paste.
Pass the paste through a fine-mesh strainer. Discard the rough residue. Heat butter in a pan over medium-high heat. Once the butter is melted, add Kashmiri red chili powder and saute for 2-3 seconds. Adding red chili powder to hot butter gives a lovely color to the curry. Add the cashew nut paste to the pan along with ½ cup water and cook for 4-5 minutes. Now add salt, ketchup, khoya, processed cheese, heavy cream, Kasuri methi, honey, and lime juice to the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes. Remove the chicken from the oven and add it to the pan. Garnish with cream swirls and serve hot.


Makki ki Roti

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups Makki ka Atta/ Corn Meal
  • 1 teaspoon Salt
  • 2 tablespoon Oil

RECIPE:

Mix makki ka atta, salt and vegetable oil in a bowl with your finger tips till everything is nicely combined. Add warm water and knead a soft dough.
This dough will behave different than your regular wheat flour dough as their is no gluten in maize flour.
Cover and keep the dough aside for 10-15 minutes.
Heat a griddle. Take two sheets of parchment paper.
Divide the dough in 8-10 equal parts. take one part of the dough and add a few more drops of water to it and knead once more for a few seconds. Keep the dough ball on one of the parchment paper and cover with another.
Gently roll the dough in to a 4-5 inch disc. Remove the top parchment and flip the roti along with the bottom parchment on the hot griddle.
Gently remove the parchment. Cook the roti from both the sides till brown spots appear.
Hold the roti with a tong and cook it on direct flame from both the sides till nicely browned. Apply ghee on top.
Serve hot with white butter, jaggery and sarson ka saag
